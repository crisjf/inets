Industry Space for Swedish cities

The network is constructed by first calculating a matrix connecting regions and industries by year. This matrix is calculated using the Revealed Comparative Advantage (RCA):

RCAir(t)=Xir(t)XXi(t)Xr(t)

and taking only those values where RCA is greater than one:

Mir(t)=1RCAir(t)>10otherwise

This enables us to define the total (accross time) number of regions in which two industries are present:

Rii'=∑t∑rMir(t)Mi'r(t)

and the total number of region in which one of the industries is present:

Ri=∑t∑rMir(t)

Next, we calculate the number of years in which an industry is present in our dataset:

yi=∑tI(Ri(t)>1)

We define the measure of relatedness as the residual of the regression:

log(Rii')=β0+β1log(RiRi')+β2log(minyi,yi')+γii'

In other words, we are considering two industries to be connected when they tend to co-locate more than what can be explained by their geographical concentration (Ri) , and their temporal concentration (yi)
